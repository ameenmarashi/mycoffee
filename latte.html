Latte: The Creamy Comfort

A latte (short for caffè latte) is a coffee drink made with espresso and steamed milk, traditionally topped with a thin layer of foamed milk. It's known for its creamy texture and milder coffee flavor compared to a cappuccino, as it contains more milk.

How to Make a Latte:

Pro Tip: Experiment with different milk types! Oat milk and almond milk can create surprisingly good foam for lattes.

  1. Prepare Your Espresso

    Pull a fresh shot (or two) of espresso directly into your serving mug or glass. This forms the base of your latte.

  2. Steam the Milk

    Fill your milk pitcher with cold milk (dairy or non-dairy). Steam the milk using your espresso machine's wand, aiming for a creamy texture with minimal large bubbles and a temperature of 140-150°F (60-65°C).

  3. Swirl and Pour

    Gently swirl the steamed milk to integrate the foam. Pour the milk steadily into the espresso, aiming for the center. As the cup fills, tilt it slightly to encourage latte art if desired.

  4. Serve Warm

    Serve immediately and enjoy the smooth, milky goodness of your homemade latte!
