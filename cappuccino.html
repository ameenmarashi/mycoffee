Cappuccino: The Classic Trio

A cappuccino is an Italian coffee drink that is traditionally prepared with double espresso, hot milk, and steamed milk foam on top. It is similar to a latte, but has a higher proportion of foam, giving it a lighter and airier texture, and a stronger coffee flavor.

How to Make a Cappuccino:

Pro Tip: For classic latte art, aim for perfectly steamed milk that is glossy and flows like wet paint.

  1. Brew Your Espresso

    Prepare a single or double shot of fresh espresso directly into a pre-warmed cappuccino cup (typically 5-6 oz capacity).

  2. Steam the Milk

    Steam cold milk to create both hot milk and a significant amount of thick, velvety foam. The milk should be sweet and silky, reaching about 140-150°F (60-65°C).

  3. Pour the Milk

    Gently swirl the steamed milk to integrate the foam. Pour the hot milk into the espresso, holding back the foam initially with a spoon. Once the cup is about two-thirds full, spoon the thick foam on top to complete the cappuccino.

  4. Garnish (Optional)

    Optionally, sprinkle a little cocoa powder or cinnamon on top for added flavor and presentation. Serve immediately.
