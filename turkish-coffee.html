Turkish Coffee: The Traditional Brew

Turkish coffee is a method of brewing coffee that is popular in Turkey and the Middle East. It consists of very finely ground coffee, boiled in water (sometimes with sugar) in a special pot called a cezve.

How to Make Turkish Coffee:

Pro Tip: Never stir Turkish coffee once it starts brewing on the heat. This helps maintain the foam and allows the grounds to settle properly.

  1. Measure Your Coffee and Water

    Use about one to two teaspoons of finely ground coffee per cup of water. Adjust to taste.

  2. Combine Ingredients

    In the cezve, add the coffee, water, and sugar if desired. Stir to combine.

  3. Heat Slowly

    Place the cezve on low heat and allow it to heat slowly without boiling. As it heats, the coffee will begin to foam.

  4. Watch for Foam

    When the coffee foams and begins to rise, remove it from the heat briefly to prevent spilling. Repeat this step as necessary to develop a rich foam.

  5. Pour and Serve

    Carefully pour the coffee into small cups, allowing the grounds to settle at the bottom. Serve hot.
