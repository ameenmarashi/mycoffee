Arabic Coffee: A Cultural Brew

Arabic coffee, also known as “Gahwa,” is a traditional coffee drink often flavored with spices and served in small cups. It is an integral part of hospitality in Arab culture.

How to Make Arabic Coffee:

Pro Tip: The amount of cardamom can drastically change the flavor profile. Start with a small amount and adjust to your personal preference.

  1. Grind Your Coffee

    Use lightly roasted Arabica beans, grinding them to a fine consistency similar to that of Turkish coffee.

  2. Boil Water

    Pour water into a pot and bring it to a boil, then add the coffee grounds.

  3. Add Spices

    Incorporate spices like cardamom, cinnamon, or saffron according to your preference.

  4. Simmer

    Reduce heat and let it simmer for a few minutes to infuse the flavors.

  5. Serve

    Pour into small cups and serve with dates or sweets for an authentic experience.
